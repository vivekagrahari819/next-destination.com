a changing country

Prime Minister Narendra Modi has changed the image of India completely in the global level. Earlier, India was thought of as a country of snake charmers. Now, it is seen as a global power, Sri Vachanananda Swami, seer of the Panchamasali Peetha in Harihar, said on Sunday. He said that it was only during Mr. Modi’s governance that the world is taking proper notice of India and its right place in the world. Mr. Modi has also done more than all earlier Prime Ministers to uphold the spirit of Hinduism, he said. Mr. Modi has taken up the task of cleaning the Ganga. “We expect him to do the same for all rivers in the country. We hope the Union Government takes up the cleaning of the Tungabhadra also,” he said. He said that he had appealed to the former Chief Minister B.S. Yeddiyurappa to organise Tungabhadra Arati. We are thankful to Chief Minister Basavaraj Bommai for sanctioning funds for the project, he said. He appealed to the Chief Minister to develop Harihar into a tourist attraction and introduce boating, create trekking trails and other facilities for tourists.

Indian food is different from rest of the world not only in taste but also in cooking methods. It reflects a perfect blend of various cultures and ages. Just like Indian culture, food in India has also been influenced by various civilizations, which have contributed their share in its overall development and the present form. India has a rich culinary heritage. Indian cuisine has a 5000-year-old history. Mythological beliefs and traditions have played an important role in its evolution and development. The main Indian food was based on Hinduism but the Islamic influence during the Mughal era, the Persian exchanges with the Middle East and the British colonial system have all contributed immensely towards laying the foundation of the Indian cuisine.
